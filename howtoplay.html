Trio consists of 49 colorful square cards numbered from 1 to 9, and 50 blue round cards numbered from 1 to 50. Colors have no effect on game rules.

Game Setup

Square cards are laid out in a 7x7 grid. Blue round cards stay in the box face down.

How to Play?

A player draws one blue card from the box and reveals it to everyone. All players try to find a calculation that reaches this number. The calculation must be made up of 3 adjacent square cards (horizontally, vertically or diagonally). The mathematical operation order is: multiplication/division first, then addition/subtraction.

The first player to find a correct operation shouts "Trio!" and announces the operation. If it’s correct, they win the blue card. If it’s wrong, the turn passes to the next player or, if there are 2 players, the turn is skipped.

The player with the most blue cards at the end wins the game.
