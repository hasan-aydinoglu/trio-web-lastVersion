TRIO - Instructions

Trio consists of 49 square colourful cards featuring the numbers from 1 to 9 as well as of 50 round blue cards featuring the numbers from 1 to 50. The colours are of no significance.

Preparing the game

The square cards are placed face up in a square of 7 by 7 cards.

The round cards remain face down in the box. The first player takes a blue card from the box and places it face up on the table, where it is clearly visible to all players.

How to play

Now all players try to think of a calculation that results in the number on the blue card. The calculation must be formed with numbers that appear on three neighbouring square cards which are aligned either horizontally, vertically or diagonally. The mathematical rule such as "multiplication or division of the first cards, then addition or subtraction of the third card" must be taken into consideration.

As soon as a player spots a correct sequence they shout "Trio!" and must then immediately demonstrate their solution. If it is correct, they add the blue card to their stack. If it is incorrect they either forfeit the round (with two players) or the round continues until one of the remaining players finds a solution.

The player who acquires most blue cards wins the game.
